**Pitch deck — subcommittee working draft**
Last updated 2026-04-23. All prices verified against live retail listings; sources linked inline.

## Slide 1 — The Pitch

A private, instructor-led bourbon and whiskey tasting positioned as a high-end DakCUPAC fundraiser for credit union executives, board members, and CU-affiliated PAC donors.

- **90 minutes**, six guided pours
- **40 attendees**, $250/ticket = **$10,000 gross**
- **Scott "Fuzzy" Meske** leads the tasting — history, mash bills, distillery stories, structured nosing
- **Custom engraved Glencairn glass** gifted at the door, doubles as the tasting glass and take-home keepsake
- **Chick-fil-A** catering — recognizable, easy, no-fuss
- **Six CU sponsors** each cover one bottle; their name sits next to the pour they sponsored
- Optional 7th "host pour" of **Heart River Spirits Painted Canyon Bourbon** (Bismarck craft distiller) for the ND angle

---

## Slide 2 — Why It Works

**Right audience for the price point.** Credit union executives and board members are exactly the demographic who give at the federal/state PAC contribution ceiling. $250 is a bourbon-tasting price, not a "fundraiser ask" price — that framing matters.

**Sponsorship eats the biggest cost line.** Bottles are the hardest budget item. Pushing them to CU sponsors means PAC keeps the gate.

**The glass is the marketing.** Every attendee leaves with an engraved Glencairn that lives in their bar at home. DakCUPAC's name shows up every time they pour a whiskey for the next decade.

**Scott is a force multiplier.** A guided tasting is not the same product as "open bar with bourbon." It justifies the ticket price and gives the room a shared experience.

## Slide 9 — Reality Check on Bottle Sourcing

**The original "$500/bottle, six bottles, ~$3,000 total" assumption needs honest revision.**

**At MSRP** (if sourced through allocations or a friendly retailer):
- Bottles 1–3 (BTAC + Pappy): $540 combined MSRP
- Bottles 4–5 (Elijah Craig 18, Michter's 10): $375 combined MSRP
- Bottle 6 (Macallan 18): ~$420 retail
- **Total at MSRP: ~$1,335 for the full flight**

**At actual street/secondary pricing** (what a CU sponsor would actually pay if they walked into Williquors and bought one):
- Bottles 1–3: **$5,000–$10,500**
- Bottles 4–5: $540–$640
- Bottle 6: $420
- **Total at street: $6,000–$11,500 for the full flight**

**Implication for the sponsorship ask:**

Two viable paths forward —

1. **Source through allocations.** Ask each sponsoring CU to put their hand up for one BTAC or Pappy lottery slot. Cost stays near MSRP (~$150–240/bottle) but sourcing depends on luck. Best handled by pooling allocations across the six CUs and Scott's industry contacts.
2. **Ask sponsors for "$500–$750/bottle" and substitute the unobtainable BTAC slots** with deeply credible, in-stock alternates: Weller 12, Blanton's Gold, Stagg Jr., Four Roses LE, Knob Creek 25th Anniversary. Less mythic on paper, far more achievable, the room still gets a knockout flight.

**Recommend path 2 as the planning baseline**, with path 1 as upside if any sponsoring CU lands a BTAC allocation. Either way, surface this honestly with Scott before publishing the bottle list.

## Slide 10 — Sponsorship Structure

**Six "Bottle Sponsor" slots — one per pour.**

Each sponsoring CU receives:
- **Tent card / placard** displayed next to their bottle on the tasting table — "This pour proudly sponsored by [CU Name + logo]"
- **Verbal callout from Scott** when that pour is introduced
- **Recognition on the printed tasting menu** handed to every guest
- **Logo on event signage** at check-in
- Two **complimentary tickets** to the event (worth $500)

**Sponsor ask (path 2 baseline):** $750/CU × 6 = $4,500 in sponsorship value, fully consumable.

**Optional add-on tier — "Featured ND Pour":**
- One sponsor underwrites the Heart River Painted Canyon host pour at a lower buy-in (**$300–500**)
- Strong fit for an ND-headquartered CU that wants a regional story attached
- One complimentary ticket included
